Description

NRV is a valve that normally allows fluid (liquid or gas) to flow through it in only one direction.

NRV also called as NRV are two-port valves, meaning they have two openings in the body, one for fluid to enter and the other for fluid to leave. There are various types of NRV used in a wide variety of applications. NRV are often part of common household items. Although they are available in a wide range of sizes and costs, NRV generally are very small, simple, or inexpensive. Check valves work automatically and most are not controlled by a person or any external control; accordingly, most do not have any valve handle or stem. The bodies (external shells) of most NRV are made of plastic or metal.

An important concept in NRV is the cracking pressure which is the minimum differential upstream pressure between inlet and outlet at which the valve will operate. Typically the NRV is designed for and can therefore be specified for a specific cracking pressure.

Heart valves are essentially inlet and outlet NRV for the heart ventricles, since the ventricles act as pumps.
